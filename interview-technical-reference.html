SIEM Design & Detection Strategy

Question10-20 Second Answer
"What's detection-driven migration?" "Instead of porting every legacy rule 1-to-1, we evaluate detection intent. XDR handles endpoint, identity, email natively â€” suspicious PowerShell, LSASS dumps, phishing â€” those get retired. Sentinel handles what XDR can't see â€” terminated employee data access, cross-domain correlation, business logic. Result: 780 rules retired to XDR, 485 purpose-built Sentinel rules."
"Why XDR-first?" "XDR has kernel-level visibility, AMSI integration, and ML-based detection that SIEM queries can't match. Example: LSASS credential dumping â€” MDE sees the memory access attempt before any log is written. PowerShell â€” AMSI shows decoded script content. Phishing â€” MDO detonates attachments in sandbox. You configure controls, you don't write malware rules. Microsoft owns detection logic, we own trust decisions."
"Sentinel vs Splunk difference?" "Splunk is SIEM-first â€” you ingest everything, build all detections yourself. Microsoft is XDR-first â€” correlation happens upstream, Sentinel fills gaps. It's not feature comparison, it's architectural difference."
"What does Splunk ES add?" "ES is the security brain on top of Splunk Enterprise. Adds prebuilt correlation searches, CIM enforcement, risk-based alerting, SOC workflows, and threat intel framework. You're paying for content maturity."
"Single workspace or multiple?" "Single workspace, multi-region DCRs for data residency. Simpler RBAC, unified correlation, easier management. Complexity is the enemy of security."
"How do you handle cost?" "Detection-driven ingestion â€” don't ingest logs you won't detect on. Storage tiers for compliance data. Commitment tiers for 50% savings. E5 grants 5MB/user/day free. Design for cost from Day 1."
