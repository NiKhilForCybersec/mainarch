Why DLP Started Later

Different Timeline, Different Dependencies

Unlike SIEM (log ingestion) or EDR (agent deployment), DLP depends heavily on data classification framework and business alignment.

Before policies can be enforced ‚Äî or even meaningfully tested ‚Äî the organization needs:

  • Data classification taxonomy agreed with business
  • Sensitive information types (SITs) defined and validated
  • Business process flows documented (what data moves where)
  • Executive buy-in on enforcement approach

Result: DLP was introduced later in the program and initially operated in audit mode while classification and policy foundations were finalized.

Why DLP Migration is Different

Unlike SIEM (backend) or EDR (silent), DLP directly impacts user workflows. Users see notifications, get blocked, and must change behavior. This makes DLP the most change-management intensive track.
