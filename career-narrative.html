Interview-Ready Response: Day-to-Day

What Do You Do Day-to-Day?
"For the last couple of months, my current responsibilities have been 
focused on supporting the DLP track, primarily in audit mode. This work 
involves reviewing DLP events, validating policy behavior against real 
business workflows, identifying false positives, and tuning conditions 
and exclusions before any enforcement decisions are made. My involvement 
here is mainly around validation and stabilization rather than leading 
the overall DLP program.

Before this recent DLP phase, my primary responsibilities were on the 
SIEM and EDR tracks. During the SIEM discovery and foundation phase, 
I designed the log ingestion strategy around an XDR-first model ‚Äî 
understanding that Defender products detect attacks while Entra ID logs 
capture control-plane changes. This drove decisions about what to ingest: 
XDR alerts and incidents flow to Sentinel for correlation, while raw MDE 
telemetry stays in XDR for Advanced Hunting. Entra ID AuditLogs and 
SigninLogs are separately ingested because XDR doesn't provide identity 
control-plane visibility.

In parallel, once the SIEM foundation was established, I was involved 
in the Microsoft Defender for Endpoint rollout. This included agent 
onboarding across 9,000 endpoints, policy configuration, ASR rules, 
exclusions, and ensuring XDR integration was properly feeding Sentinel 
with unified attack detection.

As the SIEM and MDE environments reached a stable operational state, 
my focus shifted temporarily to supporting the DLP audit phase.

Across all phases, my role has been to ensure security platforms are 
deployed in a controlled and scalable way, telemetry is reliable, and 
controls are tuned in close collaboration with SOC teams and business 
stakeholders."

Common Follow-Up Questions

QuestionResponse Approach
"Won't you miss the depth of engineering?" Consulting still requires technical depth ‚Äî you can't advise on architecture without understanding implementation details. The difference is breadth + depth across multiple clients.
"How do you handle not seeing projects through?" The goal is to enable clients to be self-sufficient. Success is measured by their capability after you leave, not your continued presence.
"What if you join a client that's a mess?" That's actually the most valuable consulting work ‚Äî helping organizations that need guidance the most. Framework thinking helps prioritize what to fix first.
"How quickly can you ramp up on new environments?" The framework (Business ‚Üí Planes ‚Üí Assets ‚Üí Risks ‚Üí Controls ‚Üí Telemetry ‚Üí Detections) is universal. Specifics change, structure doesn't. I can map any organization within days.
