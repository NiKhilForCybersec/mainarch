Parallel Operations

Managing 6+ security consoles simultaneously during the 12-month transition period. Procedures for alert handling, incident management, and console prioritization.

Why Parallel Operations?

We run legacy and new platforms in parallel during transition. This approach:

  • Avoids blind spots — if one platform misses something, the other catches it
  • Validates detection parity — compare alerts side by side
  • Enables safe SOC transition — analysts can fall back if needed
  • Reduces operational risk — no "big bang" cutover

Key insight: Rushing cutover to save parallel licensing costs is a false economy. The risk of missing detections far outweighs the license overlap cost.

Deduplication Procedure

When the same event generates alerts in multiple consoles:

  1. Acknowledge in primary console first
  2. Open corresponding alert in secondary console
  3. Link or reference (add incident number in notes)
  4. Investigate in primary only
  5. Close both with same resolution and notes
Avoid Double Investigation

Two analysts investigating the same alert in different consoles wastes time. The shift lead assigns ownership in the primary console; secondary console alerts are acknowledged as "duplicate."
