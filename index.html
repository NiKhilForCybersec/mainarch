Enterprise Security Transformation

A multi-year enterprise security transformation for RevFlow Analytics (Siemens subsidiary). Identity and Infrastructure migrations started first as cloud foundation. Security tracks followed: SIEM and EDR at M1, then DLP at M6 (delayed due to legal constraints). Currently in final handover phase with knowledge transfer to internal security team.

Confidentiality Note

The acquisition was still in progress at the time and had not been publicly announced, so I can't share specific details.

Company name and certain specifics have been generalized. Technical approaches and methodologies remain accurate.

Current State

All tracks complete. Sentinel primary (RSA decommissioned). MDE fully deployed (ESET removed). DLP enforcement active (Symantec EDLP decommissioned).

XDR-First Architecture: M365 Defender XDR enabled from Day 1. XDR handles endpoint, identity, email, and cloud app detection (780 techniques retired from RSA). Sentinel owns network, cloud, SaaS, DLP, and higher-order correlation (485 rules = 255 migrated + 230 new for coverage gaps RSA never had).

Unified SOC Interface: Analysts work in security.microsoft.com ‚Äî both XDR and Sentinel incidents appear in a single queue.

Current Phase: Knowledge transfer and final handover to internal security team.

My Role (Security Engineer ‚Äî SIEM & EDR Tracks)

Context: This was a larger transformation program with multiple workstreams. Identity and Infrastructure migrations had already started before I joined ‚Äî they were the cloud foundation that needed to be in place first. I joined when the security tracks began and worked specifically on SIEM and EDR.

SIEM Track (M1): Log source identification, stakeholder coordination, ingestion strategy, Sentinel workspace setup, connector configuration, custom log onboarding. Also contributed to detection migration ‚Äî validating XDR coverage, enabling Content Hub rules, supporting telemetry readiness for detection engineers.

EDR Track (M1): MDE deployment, policy configuration, ASR rollout, exclusions management, coexistence handling, operational tuning across 9,000 endpoints.

DLP Track (M6+, Contributed): DLP started later due to legal alignment requirements. Contributed to Symantec Endpoint DLP ‚Üí Purview migration. Currently supporting final knowledge transfer and handover.

Note: I didn't lead the overall transformation program ‚Äî I worked on the SIEM and EDR tracks and contributed to DLP.

The Challenge

RevFlow Analytics, a Siemens subsidiary providing revenue intelligence and deal prediction analytics, operated with a legacy security stack that was increasingly misaligned with their cloud-first business model. With 4,000 employees across Canada (HQ), United States, and India, serving global clients in North America, Europe, and APAC, the existing infrastructure needed modernization:

Global Compliance Approach

EU Clients Without EU Office: GDPR requirements addressed through lawful cross-border transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses) and strong technical safeguards, rather than physical EU presence.

Single SIEM Workspace: Optimized for detection consistency and operational efficiency. Architecture can scale to regional workspaces if regulatory requirements evolve.

The Approach: XDR-First Parallel Migration

This migration follows an XDR-first approach: Microsoft 365 Defender XDR is enabled from Day 1 to handle endpoint, identity, and email detection natively, while Sentinel focuses on network, Azure, and higher-order correlation. This architectural decision avoids wasted effort building detections XDR handles better.

Key Insight: Sentinel Migrations Are Architectural

In Splunk, correlation is centralized ‚Äî all telemetry goes to the SIEM. In the Microsoft ecosystem, correlation is layered: XDR handles endpoint + identity + email correlation upstream, and Sentinel handles network + Azure + higher-order correlation.

Result: 485 Sentinel rules (255 migrated + 230 new), 12-month timeline, XDR correlation benefits from Day 1.

Strategic Sequencing

XDR Enabled Early: M365 Defender connector and incident sync enabled in Month 1. XDR provides immediate value even during MDE rollout.

Detection Ownership Defined Upfront: Instead of building 500+ rules then retiring half, we build only what Sentinel needs (485 rules = 255 migrated + 230 new coverage).

DLP Started Later: DLP depends on data classification framework and business alignment, so it was introduced later and initially operated in audit mode.

Unified SOC from Start: SOC trains on the unified Defender portal (security.microsoft.com), not separate tools.
