Enterprise Security Transformation

A multi-year enterprise security transformation for RevFlow Analytics (Siemens subsidiary). SIEM and EDR migrations started first (M1), followed by DLP (M6) which required more cross-team coordination and legal alignment. Identity and Infrastructure were pre-existing and stable. Currently in final handover phase with knowledge transfer to internal security team.

Confidentiality Note

The acquisition was still in progress at the time and had not been publicly announced, so I can't share specific details.

Company name and certain specifics have been generalized. Technical approaches and methodologies remain accurate.

Current State

All tracks complete. Sentinel primary, RSA decommissioned. MDE fully deployed, ESET removed. DLP enforcement active, Symantec decommissioned.

Current Phase: Knowledge transfer and final handover to internal security team.

Role Scope (Security Engineer)

SIEM Track (M1, Started Here): Log source identification, stakeholder coordination, ingestion strategy, Sentinel workspace setup, connector configuration, custom log onboarding. Also worked on detection engineering ‚Äî implementing built-in detection rules from the translation catalog, enabling native equivalents in Sentinel and M365 Defender.

EDR Track (M1, Track Lead): MDE deployment, policy configuration, ASR rollout, exclusions management, coexistence handling, operational tuning across 9,000 endpoints.

Parallel SIEM & EDR: Worked both tracks in parallel ‚Äî ensuring consistent telemetry flow and detection alignment across platforms.

DLP Track (M6+, Current ‚Äî Wind-Up): DLP started later due to legal constraints and cross-team dependencies. Recently moved to support final knowledge transfer and handover to internal team.

Pre-Existing (Not My Scope): Identity (AD ‚Üí Entra ID) and Infrastructure (Azure) were already stable before I joined.

The Challenge

RevFlow Analytics, a Siemens subsidiary providing revenue intelligence and deal prediction analytics, operated with a legacy security stack that was increasingly misaligned with their cloud-first business model. With 4,000 employees across Canada (HQ), United States, and India, serving global clients in North America, Europe, and APAC, the existing infrastructure needed modernization:

Global Compliance Approach

EU Clients Without EU Office: GDPR requirements addressed through lawful cross-border transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses) and strong technical safeguards, rather than physical EU presence.

Single SIEM Workspace: Optimized for detection consistency and operational efficiency. Architecture can scale to regional workspaces if regulatory requirements evolve.

The Approach: Parallel Migration

Enterprise security transformations cannot be done sequentially‚Äîwaiting 4+ years to complete each migration one at a time is impractical. Instead, all five migration tracks run in parallel with extended periods of dual operation to ensure zero security gaps.

Strategic Sequencing

MDE Started Early: After discovery, MDE rollout began immediately to establish endpoint telemetry before detection implementations. This ensured telemetry was available for validation when detections went live.

Detection Work: Threat-intent driven and mapped to target controls ‚Äî not blocked by telemetry readiness.

DLP Started Later: DLP depends on data classification framework and business alignment, so it was introduced later and initially operated in audit mode.

Parallel SOC: The SOC continued on the legacy stack while Sentinel and MDE matured.
