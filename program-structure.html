Program Structure

The transformation program consists of five parallel workstreams, each with dedicated teams, running simultaneously over 18 months. This structure enables rapid progress while managing interdependencies.

Strategic Sequencing

Why This Sequence?

MDE Rollout Early: After discovery, we started MDE rollout early to establish endpoint telemetry before detection implementations began. This ensured that by the time the detection translation catalog was finalized, sufficient telemetry was already available for validation and tuning.

Detection Work: Threat-intent driven and mapped to target controls — not blocked by telemetry readiness.

DLP Later: DLP depends heavily on data classification framework and business alignment, requiring more upfront groundwork. Introduced later, initially in audit mode while classification and policy foundations were finalized.

Parallel SOC Operations: SOC continued operating on the legacy stack (RSA NetWitness, ESET) while the new Sentinel and MDE environment matured.

Team Structure & Roles

The program requires clear separation between platform engineering (building the foundation) and detection engineering (building the rules). This structure avoids bottlenecks and enables parallel progress.

Role Scope: Security Engineer (This Documentation)

SIEM Track: Log source identification, stakeholder coordination, ingestion strategy, Sentinel workspace setup, connector configuration, custom log onboarding. Detection engineering — implemented built-in detection rules from translation catalog, enabled native Sentinel/Defender equivalents, aligned endpoint telemetry correlation.

EDR Track (Lead): MDE deployment, policy configuration, ASR rollout, exclusions management, coexistence handling, operational tuning. Ensured consistent telemetry flow and detection alignment.

Parallel Operations: Worked both SIEM and EDR tracks in parallel after MDE rollout began.

DLP Track (Supporting): Audit mode monitoring, tuning, and validation after SIEM/EDR stabilized.

Detection Handoff: Detailed custom detection development and ongoing fine-tuning handled by SOC analysts and dedicated detection engineers.

Risk Management

Legacy system dependencies discovered late Medium Medium Thorough discovery phase, buffer in timeline
Cost of Parallel Operations

Running legacy and modern systems in parallel for 12+ months is expensive. Budget estimates:

  • RSA NetWitness: ~$35,000/month × 12 = $420,000
  • ESET licenses (9,000 endpoints): ~$25,000/month × 12 = $300,000
  • Symantec DLP: ~$20,000/month × 12 = $240,000
  • Sentinel (ramping): ~$8,000-15,000/month = ~$140,000
  • MDE licenses (E5): Already included in M365 E5 licensing

Total parallel operation overhead: ~$1.1M — justified by risk mitigation for a 4,000-employee, 9,000-endpoint environment. Siemens corporate standards require zero security gaps during transition.
